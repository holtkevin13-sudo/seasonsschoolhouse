1. Welcome Letter

Dear Seasons Schoolhouse Family,

Welcome — and thank you for being here. Whether you are a new family just beginning your journey with us, or a returning family stepping into another year, I want you to know how deeply I value your presence in this community.

Seasons Schoolhouse was born out of a simple but powerful belief: that children learn best when they are engaged, that families grow stronger in community, and that the homeschool journey — while beautiful — doesn't have to be walked alone.

This handbook is your guide to how we operate, what we expect of one another, and how we care for the children and families in our circle. Please read it carefully and hold onto it as a reference throughout the year.

I am so honored to do this alongside your family.

With gratitude,
Danyelle Holt
Founder, Seasons Schoolhouse

2. Mission & Vision

Mission: To provide a warm, intentional enrichment community for homeschool families in Flagler County, where children learn through hands-on experiences and families grow through shared purpose.

Vision: A co-op where every family feels seen, every child feels capable, and every season brings something worth remembering.

Philosophy: We believe that education is not a product to be delivered, but a life to be lived. We do not rush through content — we dwell in it. We do not simply teach skills — we practice them together. Learning at Seasons Schoolhouse is relational, seasonal, and rooted in the real world.

3. Co-op Overview

What We Are

Seasons Schoolhouse is a K–8 homeschool enrichment co-op based in Flagler County, Florida. We are not a school. We do not replace your homeschool — we enrich it. Our gatherings are designed to complement any homeschool method or philosophy.

Who We Serve

Families with children in kindergarten through 8th grade who are actively homeschooling and share our vision for intentional, community-centered education.

Meeting Schedule

We meet bi-weekly (every two weeks) during the school year, September through May, with a Kick-off gathering in August and an End of Year Celebration in May. All meetings and field trips take place in Flagler County, with the exception of occasional educational field trips beyond county lines.

Current Year

We are currently accepting enrollments for the 2026–2027 school year.

4. Meeting Structure & Expectations

Each Seasons Schoolhouse gathering is a full, planned day of enrichment. Gatherings are not drop-off events — parents are expected to be present and engaged throughout.

Typical Gathering Flow

  • Welcome and opening circle
  • Seasonal activity or project (hands-on focus)
  • Group meal or snack time (community-centered)
  • Second activity block or outdoor time
  • Closing and wrap-up

What to Bring

  • Any supplies communicated in advance via Band
  • Lunch and snacks for your children
  • A willing and engaged attitude

Punctuality

We ask that families arrive on time. Our gatherings are structured and community-dependent — late arrivals can disrupt the flow for everyone. If you anticipate being late, please communicate in advance through Band.

5. Parent Participation

Seasons Schoolhouse is a cooperative — which means it runs because parents show up and contribute. Active participation is not optional; it is the foundation of everything we do together.

Expectations for All Parents

  • Attend all scheduled gatherings unless excused in advance
  • Participate actively in activities alongside your children
  • Contribute to the co-op through assigned roles (preparation, setup, cleanup, leading activities, etc.)
  • Respond to Band communications in a timely manner
  • Support the community by bringing a spirit of service and collaboration

Families who consistently do not participate may be asked to step back from the co-op. This is a community effort, and every family's contribution matters.

6. Attendance Policy

Regular attendance is essential for your family and for the health of the community. Because our gatherings are collaborative and co-operative, an absent family affects the whole group.

Excused Absences

  • Illness (please do not attend if your child or a family member is unwell — see Health Policy)
  • Family emergencies
  • Pre-communicated conflicts (notified at least one week in advance when possible)

Communication

All absences should be communicated through the Band app as early as possible. Consistent unexcused absences (three or more in a school year) may result in a conversation about continued enrollment.

7. Health & Illness Policy

The health and wellbeing of every child and family in our co-op is a shared responsibility. We ask that families take this seriously and err on the side of caution.

Please Keep Your Child Home If They Have:

  • Fever within the last 24 hours
  • Vomiting or diarrhea within the last 24 hours
  • Active, contagious illness (pink eye, strep, flu, etc.)
  • Any unexplained rash or symptoms

We understand this can be disappointing, but keeping our community healthy is an act of love and respect. Your child will be missed and welcomed back when they are well.

Medications

Parents are responsible for managing any medications their children require. Please communicate any medical needs or allergies to Danyelle before the start of the school year.

8. Child Safety & Supervision

The safety of every child is our highest priority. Because Seasons Schoolhouse is a co-op model, parents share responsibility for the supervision and safety of all children present.

General Guidelines

  • All parents are responsible for supervising their own children at all times, unless otherwise arranged
  • Children should not be left unattended at any gathering
  • Any safety concerns should be raised immediately with Danyelle
  • We maintain a safe, respectful environment for every child — unkind behavior, bullying, or unsafe conduct will be addressed promptly

Emergencies

In the event of a medical emergency, Danyelle will call 911 and notify parents immediately. All gathering locations will have a first aid kit on site. Parents are encouraged to share any relevant medical information (allergies, conditions, emergency contacts) at the start of the year.

9. Communication

All enrolled families will be added to our private Band community, which serves as our primary communication platform. Band is where you will find:

  • Gathering announcements and reminders
  • Supply lists and preparation notes
  • Group chat and community updates
  • Shared photos from gatherings
  • The live, up-to-date calendar

We ask that all families download the Band app and check it regularly. Important announcements will not be repeated via email or other channels.

For questions or concerns outside of Band, you may reach Danyelle at seasonsschoolhouse@gmail.com.

10. Code of Conduct

Seasons Schoolhouse is a place of warmth, respect, and community. We ask every family — parents and children alike — to uphold the following standards.

For Children

  • Treat every person with kindness and respect
  • Care for shared spaces and materials
  • Participate with a willing and open spirit
  • Use kind words and resolve conflicts peacefully

For Parents

  • Speak about other families and children with respect — always and in all settings
  • Support the co-op leadership and trust the process
  • Bring concerns directly to Danyelle, not to the group
  • Model the behavior and spirit you hope to see in your children

Conduct that is consistently harmful to the community — whether from a child or a parent — may result in a conversation about continued enrollment. We seek to handle all concerns with grace and directness.

11. Photo & Privacy Policy

We take the privacy of every child in our co-op seriously. Seasons Schoolhouse does not publish photos of children on our public website or social media. Photos shared within our Band community are for enrolled families only and should not be shared publicly without explicit permission from the parents of all children pictured.

Photo Guidelines

  • No photos of other families' children should be posted publicly without consent
  • Photos shared in Band are for community use only — please respect the privacy of all families
  • If you have concerns about a specific photo, please reach out to Danyelle directly

12. Fees & Participation

Details regarding co-op fees, material contributions, and any associated costs for the 2026–2027 school year will be communicated directly to enrolled families. Fees, when applicable, help cover shared materials, activity supplies, and any field trip costs.

We believe financial barriers should never prevent a family from participating. If you have concerns about fees, please speak privately with Danyelle. We will work something out together.

13. Acknowledgment

By enrolling in Seasons Schoolhouse, families agree to have read, understood, and committed to the guidelines outlined in this handbook. We ask that both parents review this document and sign the acknowledgment form provided at enrollment.

This handbook may be updated from time to time. Families will be notified of any significant changes through Band. The most current version will always be available on this page.
