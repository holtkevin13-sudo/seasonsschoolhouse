The Woman Behind the Co-op

Hello, I'm Danyelle

D
Danyelle Holt
Founder & Organizer

Wife · Mom · Homeschooler
Lover of nature, hands-on learning,
and building community.

I'm a wife and mom who began our homeschool journey two years ago — and it changed everything. Stepping away from the conventional path was both freeing and humbling, and it opened my eyes to just how many other families were walking the same road, often quietly, and often alone.

That's where Seasons Schoolhouse was born. I saw a genuine need for a gathering place — a community where moms could exhale, where kids could connect, and where learning could happen the way I've always believed it should: with dirt on your hands, curiosity in your heart, and people alongside you.

I've carried a deep passion for childhood education for as long as I can remember. Providing hands-on, practical experiences for children isn't just something I believe in — it's a calling. Watching a child understand something not because they read it in a textbook, but because they lived it, is one of the most beautiful things I know.

Seasons Schoolhouse is my small offering to this community. I'm so glad you found your way here.

What We Do & Who We Are

About the Co-op

Seasons Schoolhouse is a K–8 homeschool enrichment co-op that gathers bi-weekly during the school year (September through May). We meet as a community to learn, create, explore, and grow — guided by the rhythms of each season and the curiosity of every child.

📅

When We Meet

Bi-weekly during the school year, September through May. Each gathering is a full, intentional day of learning.

🌱

Who We Welcome

Homeschool families with children in kindergarten through 8th grade. All learning styles and backgrounds are welcome here.

🏡

Our Approach

Experiential, community-led, and rooted in real-world skills. We learn by doing — not just listening.

🍂

Our Rhythm

Each season brings a new focus and fresh activities. We follow nature's lead and let the calendar shape our curriculum.

What We Explore

Learning Areas & Activities

🌿

Nature Study

Seasonal observation, plant identification, wildlife exploration, and outdoor stewardship.

🧵

Life Skills

Cooking from scratch, basic sewing, gardening, and the practical arts of everyday living.

📜

History & Humanities

Living history lessons, storytelling, cultural studies, and connection to the past.

🎨

Art & Creative Projects

Watercolor, nature journaling, crafts, and seasonal projects made with care by hand.

🚌

Field Trips

Local adventures to farms, museums, nature preserves, historical sites, and beyond.

🤝

Community Service

Teaching children to look outward — giving back and serving those around us with purpose.

Past Seasons

A look at what we've done together

Fall 2024

Harvest & Heritage

We explored the history of the harvest season, learned to can and preserve food, made apple butter from scratch, and took a field trip to a local farm to connect our learning to the land.

Winter 2025

Warmth & Craft

We dove into textile arts — basic hand sewing, weaving, and warm-weather crafts — alongside winter nature journaling and a study of how communities around the world celebrate the cold months.

Spring 2025

Seeds & Stories

Children planted their own seedlings, studied plant life cycles, explored local native plants on a guided nature walk, and worked on a community garden contribution project together.

Fall 2025

Maps, Makers & Milestones

A history-rich season exploring early American life, cartography, pioneer skills, and a field trip to a local historical site. Kids made their own hand-drawn maps and learned butter churning along the way.
